Research and Context

  • Goal: Increase the conversion rate to paid subscriptions by removing user reviews from all feature-specific paywalls. An experiment with reviews on tour install showed that excluding reviews can show better conversion rates than the review option. Based on this, let's repeat this experiment on feature paywalls
  • Foundation: Reviews on paywalls may distract users from the purchase button. Removing them keeps users focused on completing the purchase.

Research results

  • The Tour Install experiment, where user reviews were removed from the paywall, demonstrated a measurable increase in conversion rates
  • Companies like Simply Guitar and Duolingo have simplified their paywalls by minimizing distractions such as reviews, focusing on clear CTAs, and reported improved conversion rates as a result.
  • Studies in UX and CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization) show that reducing cognitive load and distractions on paywalls improves the likelihood of completing a purchase. Removing reviews eliminates unnecessary decision-making friction, as seen in the prior experiment.

Hypothesis

If we remove user reviews from feature-specific paywalls for all users during payment scenarios, we anticipate a 10% increase in banner → access conversion in 7-day retention for new users or active days for existing users. This is because simplifying the paywall by reducing distractions will keep users focused on completing the purchase. The significance level is set at 5%.
